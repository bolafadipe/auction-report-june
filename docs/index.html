Introduction

The June 2024 New York Watch Auctions marked a significant moment in the luxury timepiece market, showcasing an exceptional collection of watches from renowned brands and attracting global attention. This report delivers a detailed analysis of the auction outcomes, highlighting the distribution of sales across various dimensions including brands, models, case materials, and bracelet types. By examining data from two prominent auction houses—Phillips and Christie's—we offer valuable insights into current market dynamics, emerging trends, and the performance of leading watch brands during this pivotal month.

Methodology

The data presented in this report was collected from auction results published by Phillips and Christie's, covering the period of the September 2024 New York auction season. Our comprehensive breakdown encompasses total lots offered, overall sales figures, and achieved sell-through rates, providing a nuanced understanding of the market's strength and buyer preferences. Additionally, this report identifies standout timepieces that garnered significant interest, reflecting evolving collector tastes and investment patterns in the luxury watch sector. Through this analysis, we aim to equip watch enthusiasts, industry professionals, and market observers with crucial information to navigate the complex and ever-changing landscape of high-end watch auctions

Limitations

Due to our inability to source some of the data used in the report, we were unable to provide a full replica of the original report. As such, differences will exist between our results and those of the original report.

Brand Dominance

Rolex and Patek Philippe are the two undisputed leaders in terms of total sales, with Rolex narrowly outpacing Patek Philippe by a slight margin. Their combined sales account for nearly half of the total sales among the top 10 brands. Rolex led the auction sales with an impressive total of $5.5 million, closely followed by Patek Philippe with $5.3 million. F.P. Journe also had a significant showing, contributing over $3 million in total sales. Cartier and Audemars Piguet made up the top five, confirming their strong demand in the luxury watch market. The data also reveals that the market for vintage and independent watch brands, like Philippe Dufour and Charles Frodsham, remains robust among collectors.

Key Reasons for Their Dominance:

  • Market Reputation: Both Rolex and Patek Philippe have solidified their position as the epitome of luxury and craftsmanship over decades. Rolex, known for its precision, durability, and timeless design, appeals to both experienced collectors and newcomers. Patek Philippe’s reputation is deeply rooted in tradition and the artisanal quality of its timepieces, making it a favorite among those seeking heritage and legacy.
  • Historical Prestige and Collector Demand: Patek Philippe's rarity and exclusivity, combined with its generational watchmaking expertise, makes it a popular choice for elite collectors. Rolex, on the other hand, has an extensive history with its iconic models (e.g., Daytona, Submariner) that drive demand from both collectors and aspirational buyers.
  • Brand Desirability: These two brands have built global desirability. Rolex, with its sport-luxury appeal, is often considered a symbol of success, while Patek Philippe is associated with exclusivity and refined taste.

Combined Sales Comparison: With total sales of over 10M USD between them, Rolex and Patek Philippe dominate the market. Their combined sales overshadow those of all other brands, demonstrating the broad market appeal these two brands command. Their ability to capture both the high-end collector’s market and casual luxury buyers makes them industry leaders in terms of both volume and value.

Insights on the Luxury Watch Market

The sales data indicate several clear trends within the luxury watch market:

  • Heritage and Brand Legacy Matter: The dominance of brands like Rolex and Patek Philippe shows a strong preference for classic, long-established brands with a proven track record of delivering value and prestige. The consistent performance of F.P. Journe, Audemars Piguet, and Cartier further emphasizes the appeal of well-established brands with deep market penetration.
  • Independent Watchmaking on the Rise: The presence of smaller, independent brands such as Philippe Dufour and Richard Mille highlights growing demand for watches that offer uniqueness and craftsmanship over mass production. Collectors increasingly value bespoke watchmaking, with independent artisans gaining a stronger foothold in the auction market.
  • Rarity and Innovation Drive Premium Prices: Brands with limited production numbers or groundbreaking innovation tend to capture higher prices at auction. This is particularly true for F.P. Journe, Richard Mille, and Philippe Dufour, where scarcity and innovative design make them highly sought after.

Potential Growth Areas

Based on current performance, several brands show potential for future growth:

  • Richard Mille: Its combination of futuristic design, innovative materials, and celebrity endorsements positions it for continued growth. If it can maintain its momentum in the luxury space, it could challenge traditional brands in future auctions.
  • Philippe Dufour and Charles Frodsham: These independent watchmakers, while operating on a small scale, could see their value rise as collectors increasingly seek unique, hand-made timepieces. Growing demand for independent brands in the luxury watch market indicates that niche watchmakers could see significant appreciation in future auctions.
  • Audemars Piguet and A. Lange & Söhne: Both brands have the pedigree and craftsmanship to continue growing their market share. A focus on limited-edition collections and complicated timepieces could further bolster their standing in the auction market.

Top Case Sizes Analysis

Case Size Dominance

The 40mm case size dominated total sales, reaching nearly 4 million USD in total sales, significantly outpacing all other case sizes. This dominance indicated a strong preference among collectors and buyers for watches with a versatile, classic size that appealed to a broad range of wrist sizes.

Other popular case sizes included 42mm and 37mm, with total sales reaching approximately 2.8 million USD and 2.5 million USD, respectively. These sizes reflected a balance between modern trends favoring slightly larger watches and the enduring appeal of more traditionally sized timepieces.

Key Reasons for the Dominance of 40mm:

  • Versatility and Comfort: The 40mm case size was widely regarded as an ideal watch size for a range of wrist sizes, making it a popular choice among both men and women. It was large enough to be legible and modern without being oversized.
  • Iconic Models: Many iconic watch models, such as the Rolex Submariner and Cosmograph Daytona, were produced in the 40mm size, driving its popularity at auctions. These highly sought-after models likely contributed significantly to the overall sales figures for this case size.
  • Historical Preference: Historically, 40mm had been a standard size for luxury watches, offering a perfect blend of elegance and practicality. This long-standing popularity continued to influence buyer preferences.

Variety in Case Sizes

The chart showed a clear preference for mid-sized watches, with sizes ranging from 35mm to 42mm dominating the top sales figures. While 40mm led by a significant margin, there was substantial interest in sizes just above and below it, such as 42mm, 39mm, and 37mm.

Interestingly, smaller sizes such as 35mm and 36.5mm still held relevance in the luxury watch market, likely appealing to buyers who preferred more classic or vintage-inspired designs, or possibly representing women's watch models.

Insights on the Luxury Watch Market

The data reflected a market that primarily favored moderately sized timepieces, with a clear preference for watches between 37mm and 42mm. This range suggested a balance between modern tastes and traditional aesthetics.

  • Dominance of Classic Sizes: The strong performance of 40mm, 42mm, and 37mm watches demonstrated the enduring appeal of classic and versatile sizes that suit a wide range of preferences and wrist sizes.
  • Gradual Shift Towards Larger Sizes: While not extreme, the presence of 41mm and 42mm in the top-selling sizes indicated a slight trend towards larger watches, possibly reflecting changing consumer preferences or the influence of sports and diving watch designs.
  • Continued Relevance of Smaller Sizes: The inclusion of sizes from 35mm to 37.5mm in the chart highlighted a persistent market for more traditionally sized watches, likely driven by vintage-inspired designs and preferences for understated elegance.

Potential Growth Areas

Looking ahead, several trends may shape future sales by case size:

  • Continued Dominance of 40mm: Given its overwhelming lead in sales, the 40mm size was likely to remain a staple in the luxury watch market, appealing to a wide range of consumers.
  • Growing Interest in 42mm: The strong showing of 42mm suggested potential for growth in slightly larger watches that offer a more prominent wrist presence without being overly large.
  • Resurgence of Traditional Sizes: The significant sales of 37mm and 38mm watches indicated a continued appreciation for more traditionally sized timepieces, which could see increased demand as vintage-inspired designs gain popularity.
  • Niche Opportunities in Diverse Sizes: The presence of sizes ranging from 35mm to 42mm suggested opportunities for watchmakers to cater to specific tastes and demographics across a variety of case sizes.

Top Case Materials Analysis

Case Material Dominance

The data shows that 18K yellow gold leads the total sales with nearly 5M USD, followed closely by stainless steel at just over 4.7M USD and platinum at 4.17M USD. This dominance by gold and platinum materials reflects the luxury market's preference for precious metals in high-end timepieces, emphasizing the association of these materials with wealth, status, and exclusivity.

The case materials used in high-end watches are key factors in determining their market value. Watches made from 18K white gold and 18K pink gold also perform well in the auction market, contributing to their high standing in the top 10.

Key Reasons for the Dominance of Precious Metals:

  • Luxury Appeal: Gold (yellow, white, and pink) and platinum are universally associated with luxury and exclusivity, which attracts high-end collectors. These materials not only add aesthetic value but are also seen as symbols of wealth and prestige, making them highly desirable in the luxury watch market.
  • Durability and Value Retention: Both platinum and gold are known for their durability and ability to retain value over time. These materials age well and often improve in worth, which is important to collectors looking to invest in watches as tangible assets.
  • Historical Significance: Many iconic and vintage timepieces are crafted from gold or platinum, making these materials historically significant. Their longstanding use in luxury watchmaking contributes to their ongoing popularity at auctions.

The combined sales of precious metal materials far outstrip those of other materials, indicating that collectors and buyers continue to place significant value on traditional luxury materials.

Variety in Case Materials

Beyond the top three materials, other metals such as 18K white gold and 18K pink gold also show substantial sales figures, contributing significantly to the overall market. This highlights the appeal of different types of gold to collectors who value aesthetic variety and craftsmanship in their timepieces.

Less traditional materials like Carbon TPT and white ceramic make an appearance in the top 10, showing that innovative materials are gaining traction among luxury watch buyers. These materials appeal to collectors looking for something beyond the conventional, whether for technical reasons (such as lightweight properties) or unique aesthetics.

Insights on the Luxury Watch Market

The dominance of gold and platinum reinforces the classic connection between luxury watches and precious metals. However, the presence of more innovative materials suggests a growing demand for unique, modern designs that still offer premium value.

  • Precious Metals Lead the Market: Traditional materials like 18K yellow gold, stainless steel, and platinum remain top choices for high-end collectors. These materials continue to be symbols of timeless luxury and are preferred for their ability to retain and even increase in value over time.
  • Emerging Interest in Innovative Materials: The inclusion of materials like Carbon TPT and white ceramic shows that the market is not purely traditional. Collectors are increasingly drawn to innovative materials that offer unique features like lightweight durability or distinctive aesthetics.
  • Diverse Appeal Across Gold Variants: While yellow gold remains the most dominant, white gold and pink gold continue to perform well, showing a demand for both classic and slightly more modern variations of gold in high-end watches.

Potential Growth Areas

Several trends point to potential growth areas for certain materials:

  • Carbon and Ceramic Materials: As lightweight, innovative materials like Carbon TPT and ceramics gain more attention, we may see increasing demand for watches using these materials in future auctions. These materials offer collectors something fresh and modern, which could appeal to a younger, more contemporary audience.
  • Continued Strength of Precious Metals: Despite the rise of modern materials, precious metals like gold and platinum will likely continue to dominate the luxury watch market. Their historical significance, durability, and aesthetic appeal ensure their place at the top of collectors' preferences.
  • Hybrid Materials: We may see more watchmakers exploring hybrid materials that combine traditional metals with innovative materials, providing both luxury and technical innovation. These could represent a new category of highly sought-after timepieces at auctions.
